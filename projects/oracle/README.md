# Oracle Health & AI

I joined Oracle Health & AI right after undergrad, at a time when the org itself was still brand new. It felt a lot more like a startup than a large company — small teams, fast-moving projects, and the opportunity to build foundational pieces of the system from scratch.

Over my time there, I worked across a pretty wide surface area: backend APIs, cloud infrastructure, CI/CD, and eventually agent-based systems. Early on, I was focused on Java clinical services and helping stand up the core infrastructure for new hospital tenants — everything from APIs to Terraform-defined environments and deployment pipelines. I got to be a part of (and often lead) the first production deployments for new clients, which was a pretty unique experience early in my career.

As the platform evolved, so did my work. I gradually moved deeper into system design and architecture — building shared infrastructure like our configuration service and rules engine, and later focusing heavily on agentic systems. In my final year, a lot of my work centered around designing and prototyping agent-based approaches to traditionally rigid systems (like registration and rules execution), which ultimately led to some of the projects below.

Below are a few of the projects I worked on that I think best represent that progression.
